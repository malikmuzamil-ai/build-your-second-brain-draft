Data Collection

Your second brain is only as good as what you feed it. Before it can write anything, it needs to know what you do and how you talk — and those live in two different places.

Source of WHAT you do

Your LinkedIn posts

On stage. Positioned. Your offer, your clients, your results, your beliefs about the industry — the best written record of your business that exists.

Source of HOW you talk

Your sent emails

Off stage. No hooks, no performance. Just you explaining things to one real person. The closest thing to your natural voice in writing.

Workflow A · Pre-work — do this 2 days before

LinkedIn Export

Gives you: your full post history — business context and public voice in one file.

  1. On LinkedIn, click your photo (top right) → Settings & Privacy
  2. Left menu → Data Privacy → Get a copy of your data
  3. Choose "Want something in particular?" and tick Posts and Messages. Skip the full archive — that one takes a day.
  4. Click Request archive, confirm your password. LinkedIn emails you a download link in ~15 minutes.
  5. Download the .zip, unzip it, find Shares.csv — every post you have ever published.
  6. Drag Shares.csv into your vault's 00-Inbox folder. Don't open it in Excel. Don't clean it.
DONE →00-Inbox/Shares.csv is sitting in your vault, untouched.
FALLBACKArchive didn't arrive, or you have under 10 posts? Open your profile → Show all activity → Posts, copy your 10 best posts (the ones that got comments) into a blank doc, save as my-posts.md, drop it in 00-Inbox. Five minutes, same result.
Workflow B · Live on Day 1 — 5 minutes

Sent Emails via Gmail Connector

Gives you: 15–20 of your real client replies — your voice with nobody watching.

  1. In Claude (claude.ai), click the connectors icon in the chat input → find Gmail → Connect
  2. Sign in with the account you actually email clients from, approve access
  3. Run the extraction prompt below — Claude searches your sent folder and pulls only the words you wrote
  4. Download the compiled file, rename it voice-emails.md, drop it into 00-Inbox
claude.ai — Gmail connected
Search my sent emails from the last 90 days. Ignore newsletters,
calendar invites, and one-line replies under 20 words. Find 15-20
emails where I explain my work, answer a client question, handle
an objection, or describe my offer. Extract ONLY the text I wrote —
strip signatures, quoted threads, and legal footers. Compile it
into one markdown document, each email as its own section,
labeled by topic (not the recipient's name).
DONE →00-Inbox/voice-emails.md exists — your replies only, no signatures, no quoted text.
FALLBACKOn Outlook or won't connect? In Gmail search in:sent after:2026/04/01, open 15 substantive threads, copy only your replies into a doc. Ten minutes of copy-paste, guaranteed to work. Strip anything with passwords or invoices before it enters the vault.
Workflow C · Live on Day 1 — 5 minutes

Business Documents

Gives you: depth the posts don't have — proposals, offer one-pagers, sales decks, old website copy.

  1. Open Google Drive, Notion, or your desktop — wherever your business documents live
  2. Grab anything that explains your offer: proposals you've sent, a pitch deck, your website copy, an FAQ you keep answering
  3. Export or copy each into 00-Inbox — PDF, .md, .docx, all fine. Messy is fine. It lands raw.
  4. No documents at all? Record a 10-minute voice note answering: who do you help, what result did you last deliver, what do clients always ask, what does your industry get wrong, what's your offer. Transcribe it, save as brain-dump.md.
DONE →3–6 business files sitting in 00-Inbox alongside your posts and emails.

Voice DNA Extraction

One skill reads everything in your inbox and produces the two files your entire second brain stands on. This is the moment the raw data becomes you, written down.

The Voice DNA Extractor Skill

One prompt. Two foundation files.

Runs inside Claude Code, in your vault. Reads posts + emails + documents, and writes evidence-based profiles — nothing invented, everything quoted from your own words.

claude code — ~/Second Brain
Read everything in 00-Inbox: Shares.csv (my LinkedIn posts),
voice-emails.md (my sent emails), and all business documents.

TASK 1 — create Raw/voice-dna.md from my posts AND emails:
- Sentence rhythm: short and punchy or long and flowing? 2 real examples.
- Vocabulary: 10 words or phrases I use repeatedly.
- How I open: my 3 most common hook patterns, with real examples.
- How I close: what my CTAs and sign-offs sound like.
- Conviction: do I hedge or state things directly? Show proof.
- Email voice vs post voice: what changes when I write 1-on-1.
- 5 phrases that are unmistakably mine.

TASK 2 — create Raw/business-context.md from posts AND documents:
- Who I help (in the exact words I use)
- What I sell: offers, and pricing if stated anywhere
- Results I point to: every specific number or client outcome found
- What I believe my industry gets wrong
- Questions clients keep asking (from emails and FAQ)

Use ONLY evidence from my files. Do not invent patterns.
If any section lacks evidence, say what's missing instead of guessing.

Raw/voice-dna.mdHow you talk — rhythm, vocabulary, hooks, conviction, signature phrasesEvery post, DM, and email the brain will ever write for you
Raw/business-context.mdWhat you do — ICP, offer, proof, beliefs, client FAQsPositioning, content strategy, and never re-explaining yourself again
DONE →Open voice-dna.md and read it. If your reaction is "yeah, that's how I write" — it worked. If it feels generic, you fed it too few posts: add more via the fallback and rerun.

Build the Brain — Obsidian + Claude Code

Now the container. Obsidian holds the files, Claude Code does the thinking, and one shared folder connects them. Five moves and the brain is live.

Setup · ~20 minutes

The five moves

  1. Download Obsidian from obsidian.md — free, works on Mac and Windows. Install and open it.
  2. Create your vault. Click Create new vault, name it Second Brain, and save it somewhere you can find — Documents works. A vault is just a normal folder on your computer. That's the whole trick.
  3. Build the folder skeleton inside the vault: 00-Inbox (raw data lands here) · Raw (permanent sources like voice-dna.md) · Wiki (Claude's synthesized notes) · Sessions (what Claude did each run). Move your Step 1 files into 00-Inbox now if they're still on your desktop.
  4. Install Claude Code. Node.js goes in first (nodejs.org, LTS version), then in your terminal: npm install -g @anthropic-ai/claude-code. Sign in when it asks.
  5. Connect the two. In the terminal, navigate into your vault — cd Documents/Second Brain — then type claude. Claude Code now sees every file in your vault. Obsidian and Claude Code are working on the same folder: one is your window, the other is the worker.
NOTESpaces in folder names trip up terminals. If cd Second Brain fails, use cd "Second Brain" with quotes — announce this from stage before anyone hits it.
The brain's constitution

Write CLAUDE.md

The one file Claude Code reads before every session. It tells the brain who you are, where things live, and how to behave. Keep it under 500 words.

Second Brain / CLAUDE.md
# Who I am
[Name], [role]. I help [ICP] achieve [result].

# My vault
00-Inbox = unprocessed data. Raw = permanent sources.
Wiki = your synthesized notes. Sessions = log every run here.

# Standing rules
- Before writing ANY content, read Raw/voice-dna.md
  and Raw/business-context.md first.
- Never invent client results or numbers.
- Log what you did each session in Sessions/.
- Ask before deleting anything.
DONE →Test it. In Claude Code type: "Read my voice-dna.md and write one LinkedIn post about [your topic] in my voice." When the draft comes back sounding like you — your second brain is officially live.
